Single Cell Analysis

Follicular Lymphoma data analysis

By applying series of high-dimensional RNA and DNA single-cell techniques, we aim to characterize the different constituent cell types before and after Follicular Lymphoma transformation and reveal the nature of interaction between tumor cells and other cell components. We aim to describe the microenvironmental selective pressures process in order to provide rational for development of innovative immune-therapies. Using a control cohort of long-term non-transformed cases (non-tFL), we aimed to define potential predictive markers of transformation, both within tumor cells and tumor microenvironment.



Breast Cancer data analysis

We preformed the transcriptome analysis of TNBC breast cancer cell line SUM149 using Fluidigm C1 sequencing platform. Our study first revealed heterogeneous expression of marker genes (ER, PR, and HER) across the TNBC cells. Then at cellular level, we identified three cancer stem cell populations: epithelial-mesenchymal transition (EMT) CSCs, mesenchymal- epithelial transition (MET) CSCs and Dual-EMT-MET CSCs. At transcriptomic level, these cell populations also manifested a high level of heterogeneity in alternative splicing patterns, which would be neglected in an analysis of lower resolution. This is one of the leading studies to profile heterogeneity of breast cancer using Fluidigm platform at both single-cell- and single-isoform-level, pushing cell-level and isoform-level analysis to its current technical limit. (See poster in MIDAS Health Sciences Challenge Symposium for more information.)
